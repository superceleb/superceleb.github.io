How did Fernando Alonso start his Professional Career?

Fernando began go-karting at the tender age of three and subsequently won championships at the municipal, national, and international levels. Because his family lacked the financial resources to purchase him a quality kart, Alonso was compelled to compete on a damp track with slick rubber.

He won the juvenile championship for children in Asturias and Galicia. Having won both the Asturias and Basque Country Cadet Championships, he was granted admission to a class despite his youth and inability to operate heavy machinery.

He abandoned go-karting and began car racing at the age of seventeen. When requested to drive the Campos racecar, he agreed to a three-year contract with the organization.

After securing nine prime positions and six victories, he debuted in automobile racing and defeated Manual Giao to the championship in the season’s final race.

He subsequently entered the International Formula 3000 Championship, delving even further. Additionally, he is considered one of the greatest Formula One drivers of all time.

As an all-around driver, he is capable of repairing a sliding vehicle and mounting an apex to increase its speed.

Fernando has been the recipient of a multitude of nominations and accolades. His commitment is commended, and he is never disregarded.

Awarded the Lorenzo Bandini Trophy, Sporting Newcomer of the Year, and Prince Austria Sports Awards, among others, he currently retains the distinction of being the initial driver to achieve double induction into the FIA Hall of Fame.
