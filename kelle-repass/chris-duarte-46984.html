Chris Duarte- Biography

Chris Duarte is a professional basketball player from Canada who currently plays for the Indiana Pacers of the NBA (National Basketball Association). He was a college basketball player for the Northwest Florida State Raiders and the Oregon Ducks. He received the NABC NJCAA Player of the Year award in 2019 and the Jerry West Award in 2021, establishing him as a top collegiate shooting guard.

Chris Duarte- Professional Career

Chris began his career as a freshman with Northwest Florida State College, where he averaged 12.1 points per game, 6.7 rebounds, and two steals in 23.3 minutes per game. He was named All-Panhandle Conference and helped his team advance to the Elite Eight round of the NJCAA Division I Championship. He announced on September 20th, 2018, that he will continue to play for NCAA Division I program Oregon after one more season with Northwest Florida State. Duarte made his Oregon debut on November 5, 2019, when he played against Fresno State and helped the Ducks win 71-57. He rushed for 31 points and shot 12 of 15 from the field in his junior high season.

He was named Pac-12 Conference Player of the Week the next day. On January 23, 2020, he set an overall record of 30 points, 11 rebounds, eight steals, and a 79-70 victory over USC. The Indiana Pacers selected him in the 13th round of the NBA Draft in 2021. He signed his contract with the Pacers on August 4th, and he made his debut on August 9th, 2021, with a 94-86 loss to the New York Knicks. On the 20th of October, he made his NBA debut, scoring 27 points and grabbing five rebounds, helping the Pacers set a rookie debut record.
