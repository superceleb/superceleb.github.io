How did Jonathan Majors start his Professional Career?

Jonathan started as an actor by participating in stage shows. He made an appearance as “Henri Marx” in Jeff Augustin’s production of “Cry Old Kingdom,” which was staged at the “Actors Theatre of Louisville” in Kentucky from March 8 to April 7, 2013.

Soon after, on April 6-7, 2013, as part of the “Humana Ten-Minute Plays Festival,” he made an appearance opposite Andy Lucien (cast as “Laurence Fishburne”) in Josephson’s play “27 Ways I Didn’t Say “H” To Laurence Fishburne.”

He portrayed “Corporal Henry Woodson,” a soldier who worked for “Blocker” and helped to escort “Yellow Hawk” home, in the 2017 Western movie “Hostiles.” The same year, he made his television debut in “When We Rise,” an eight-part Jonathan Majors miniseries about LGBT rights activism in the country.

He played a younger version of “Ken Jones” (played by Michael K. Williams) in four episodes (played by Michael K. Williams). Then, he played Johnny “Lil Man” Curry, a local gangster and drug lord, in the 2018 biographical crime movie “White Boy Rick.”

In the English crime drama “Out of Blue,” he played “Duncan J. Reynolds,” a college professor who does research in parallel universes. He won praise for his performance as “Montgomery Allen” in Joe Talbot’s feature directorial debut, “The Last Black Man in San Francisco” (2019).

He portrayed Rafe Drummond in the crime science-fiction thriller “Captive State” and Greg in Nabil Elderkin’s debut feature film, “Gully,” both in 2019. On May 2, 2018, Jonathan was announced as “Atticus Black,” a war veteran from the 1950s searching for his father in the drama-horror movie “Lovecraft Country.”

In Spike Lee’s epic battle film “Da 5 Bloods,” which was made available on Netflix, he portrayed “David.” He gained notoriety for his role as Atticus Freeman in the HBO series Lovecraft Country. He appeared in the 2021 film The Harder They Fall, directed by Jaymes Samuel, alongside Idris Elba, Zazie Beetz, Regina King, and Delroy Lindo.
