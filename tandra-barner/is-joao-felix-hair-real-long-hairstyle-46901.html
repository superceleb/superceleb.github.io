Joao Felix stands out among football players because of his distinctive hairstyle. How natural is Joao Felix’s hair?

Joao Felix plays professional football for Portugal. He plays forward for La Liga club Atlético Madrid and the Portugal national team.

He is highly recognized for his versatility, intelligence, finishing, and dribbling in addition to his ability to play as an attacking midfielder or winger.

Félix originally trained with Porto’s youth teams before joining rivals Benfica in 2015.

A year later, he joined the latter’s reserve squad. In 2018, Felix was promoted to the first team, where he made his career debut at the age of 17.

For his efforts in the Primeira Liga, Joao won the Golden Boy and Best Young Player of the Year honors.

He was the youngest player to score a hat trick in the UEFA Europa League at the age of 19. Félix previously represented Portugal as a youth international, competing at the under-18, under-19, and under-21 levels.

When his nation won the inaugural season of the league at home in the 2019 UEFA Nations League Finals semifinals, Felix earned his first cap for the senior national team.

Joao Felix Hairstyle: Is It Natural?

Joao Felix did indeed grow his hair organically. His hairdo is the most recent trend to sweep Brazil.

It changes thick, wavy, unruly natural hair into lovely waves that emit an elegant yet edgy style with only a short snip on the temples and around the ears.

Frizzy curls will start to appear everywhere if not routinely handled by a professional, so for it to be effective you must either make an upfront investment in the proper supplies or frequently return for touch-ups after your hair grows out again.

However, the well-known hairdo of Portuguese footballer Ronaldo “Joao” Luis Nazario de Lima has recently gained popularity in Lisbon, the continent’s capital.

Joao’s haircut is also well-known in other cities with substantial Brazilian immigrant populations, such as Milan, Paris, London, and Manchester in the UK.
