N’Golo Kante is a French professional football player who mostly plays as a defensive midfielder for the English club Chelsea and the France national team. He made his senior debut with Boulogne in 2012 after beginning his junior career with JS Suresnes in 1999. He played for Leicester City from 2015 to 2016 after playing for Caen from 2013 to 2015. Kante was a crucial member of Leicester’s 2015–16 team that won the Premier League title. He won the Premier League with Chelsea in 2016–17 and the FA Cup in 2017–18. He also became the first outfield player since Eric Cantona to win back-to-back English Premier League championships with various clubs in 1992 and 1993.

Kante represented France in his first senior international match in 2016. He played a significant role in helping France finish second in the 2016 Euro Cup and take home the 2018 FIFA World Cup trophy in Russia.
